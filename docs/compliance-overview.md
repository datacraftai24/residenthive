# ResidenceHive — Compliance Overview

**Last updated:** March 29, 2026
**Jurisdiction:** Massachusetts (MA)
**Applicable to:** All consumer-facing buyer reports, property recommendations, and AI-generated content

---

## 1. Fair Housing Compliance

**Regulation:** Federal Fair Housing Act (42 U.S.C. § 3601–3619) + Massachusetts Fair Housing Act (M.G.L. c. 151B)

**What we do:**
- All agents must acknowledge Fair Housing compliance during onboarding before accessing the platform
- Massachusetts protects 14+ classes: the 7 federal protected classes (race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability) plus sexual orientation, gender identity, age, marital status, veteran status, genetic information, ancestry, and source of income
- Every buyer report includes a Fair Housing statement in the compliance footer
- The Equal Housing Opportunity (EHO) logo is displayed on every consumer-facing report
- Schools are displayed as count and distance only — no subjective quality judgments ("11 nearby schools"), never "great schools" or "top-rated district"
- AI-generated content never includes subjective commentary on neighborhoods, demographics, crime, or school quality

**AI guardrails:**
- Questions about crime rates, school quality, neighborhood safety, and demographics trigger a hard compliance freeze — the AI produces zero content and defers to the agent
- No steering language is generated by AI — the platform does not characterize neighborhoods as "good," "bad," "family-friendly," or "up-and-coming"

---

## 2. Agency Disclosure

**Regulation:** 254 CMR 3.00(13)(a) — Massachusetts Mandatory Licensee-Consumer Relationship Disclosure

**What we do:**
- Every buyer report includes an Agency Disclosure banner identifying the agent and their role
- A separate Agency Disclosure notice in the compliance footer reminds buyers that Massachusetts law requires a written disclosure form at the first meeting to discuss a specific property
- Agents acknowledge this obligation during onboarding compliance acknowledgment

---

## 3. Buyer Representation Agreement (NAR Settlement)

**Regulation:** NAR Settlement (effective August 17, 2024) — written buyer representation agreement required before touring a home

**What we do:**
- Every "Request Showing" button on the buyer report displays a visible notice: "A written buyer representation agreement is required before touring a property. Your agent will provide this agreement prior to scheduling a showing."
- Agents acknowledge this obligation during onboarding
- No showing can be initiated through ResidenceHive without the buyer seeing this notice

---

## 4. AI Transparency & Disclosure

**Basis:** Best practice + anticipated MA Bill H.81 (AI transparency disclosure for consumer-facing content)

**What we do:**
- Every buyer report includes an AI disclaimer: "This report was prepared with the assistance of AI technology. All property data is sourced from MLS and public records. AI-generated insights are for informational purposes only and are subject to review by your agent."
- AI-generated property concerns include an inline disclaimer: "AI-generated insights based on listing data and public records. Buyer should verify all property conditions independently with qualified professionals."
- AI-generated photo analysis includes a disclaimer: "Photo analysis is AI-generated and for informational purposes only. Listing photos may not reflect current property conditions."
- Agents acknowledge during onboarding that AI-generated content requires their professional judgment and does not constitute legal or real estate advice
- The AI chat assistant displays a scope limitation: it provides property information only, not legal, financial, inspection, or professional real estate advice

---

## 5. Broker Identification

**Regulation:** 254 CMR 3.00(9)(a) — all advertising of real property must include the name of the real estate broker

**What we do:**
- Every buyer report displays the brokerage legal name and MA broker license number in the compliance footer
- Agent name and contact information (phone, email) are displayed alongside the brokerage
- Reports cannot be generated or shared until brokerage compliance fields (name, license, jurisdiction) are complete — enforced at the API level

---

## 6. MLS PIN Data Compliance

**Basis:** MLS Property Information Network, Inc. vendor agreement and data display requirements

**What we do:**
- Every buyer report includes the full MLS PIN data disclaimer regarding data accuracy, source attribution, and permitted use
- A data staleness notice informs consumers that listing status, price, and availability may have changed
- The report generation date is displayed so consumers know when the data was compiled
- Property listing data is used solely for the personal, non-commercial use of consumers with a good faith interest in purchasing

---

## 7. Lead Paint Disclosure

**Regulation:** MA Lead Law (M.G.L. c. 111 §§ 190-199B) — Property Transfer Lead Paint Notification required before Purchase & Sale agreement

**What we do:**
- Properties built before 1978 display a prominent orange badge: "Built before 1978 — Lead Paint Disclosure Required"
- This badge appears only when the buyer's profile indicates they have children AND the property was built before 1978
- This is a deterministic, factual trigger (year_built + has_kids) — no AI judgment involved
- If either data point is unavailable, the badge is suppressed (no false "no risk" indication)

---

## 8. Consumer Privacy

**Basis:** MLS PIN consumer-facing platform requirements + general privacy best practices

**What we do:**
- A privacy policy is published at residencehive.com/privacy covering data collection, AI usage, MLS data, messaging integrations, and user rights
- Every buyer report links to the privacy policy in the compliance footer
- Buyer notes sections include a visibility notice: "Information you share through ResidenceHive may be included in reports provided to your agent"
- We do not sell personal information
- We do not use consumer data to train AI models

---

## 9. Consumer Protection (M.G.L. c. 93A)

**Regulation:** Massachusetts Unfair and Deceptive Practices Act

**What we do:**
- AI-generated property assessments (concerns, noise level, condition observations) include inline disclaimers that these are AI-generated and not professional inspections
- No AI-generated content is presented as a representation by the agent or brokerage
- Budget-derived comparisons (e.g., "18% under max") are clearly based on buyer-shared preferences, not independent valuations
- All property concerns advise independent verification with qualified professionals (home inspectors, attorneys, etc.)

---

## 10. Agent Onboarding Compliance

Before any agent can access the platform or generate buyer reports, they must complete a compliance acknowledgment covering four areas:

1. **Fair Housing** — jurisdiction-specific protected classes
2. **Agency Disclosure** — responsibility for 254 CMR 3.00(13)(a) mandatory disclosure form
3. **Buyer Representation** — NAR settlement requirement for written agreement before showings
4. **AI Disclosure** — acknowledgment that AI-generated content requires professional judgment

This acknowledgment is timestamped and stored. It is a hard gate — no agent can access the dashboard without completing it.

---

## 11. Report Generation Compliance Gate

Reports cannot be generated or shared unless the following conditions are met (enforced at the API level in production):

- Brokerage legal name is on file
- Brokerage jurisdiction is set (determines which compliance rules apply)
- MA broker license number is on file (for MA jurisdiction)
- Agent name is on file (used in AI disclaimer)
- Brokerage compliance setup is confirmed by administrator

If any condition is not met, the report is blocked and the agent sees a clear error message directing them to their administrator.
